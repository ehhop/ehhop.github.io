Our Vision:

To empower student-run free clinics to improve the health of

underserved individuals and communities by providing access to quality care.


Our Mission:

To be the leading resource for the establishment, development, and maintenance

of student-run free clinics based on the assessed needs unique to each community.

Who We Are, What We Do

Working to give those in need the care they deserve.


Dr. Yasmin Meah first imagined the idea of an SRFC consulting group while thinking of ways for EHHOP to help underserved communities outside of the East Harlem community. She knew that running a clinic is an incredibly challenging task; it had taken years before EHHOP was running at the level that it operates at now. The EHHOP Consulting Group would be a way for our clinic to share the knowledge it had acrued over the years with others.


At ECG, our main goal is to see our clients succeed. By working with our clients to amass information, solve problems, and build new strategies, we strive to improve the health of communities all over America. Since our conception, we've helped clinics prepare for their opening day, optimize clinic day operations, identify new funding sources, increase their community imprint, and much more.


And this is just the beginning. Every day, we're working to learn more and share that knowledge.


That's our story. Let us be a part of yours.

Select Projects:


Launching Can Be Tough

With our experience, it's a little easier.

We've aided SRFCs in getting started from ground up. Some services include helping get the medical school administration on board, clarifying medico-legal/liability issues, and providing a blueprint for clinic day operations to ensure smooth operation from day one.

Clinics Require Funding

We can help you get some.

In addition to providing one-on-one consulting services to clinics, we've also held workshops at conferences on identifying high yield grants and preparing clinic impact documents to maximize chances of acquiring grant funding.

Forging Relationshhips is Key

Work with us to identify community partners.

Integrating into the community is essential. Community stakeholders can not only refer patients to a clinic, but also help identify existing services that patients can leverage. We've aided the establishment of a link between SRFCs, a needle-exchange program, and a partnership with food pantry services.
